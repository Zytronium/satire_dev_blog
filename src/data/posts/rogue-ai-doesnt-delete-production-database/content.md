> Author's note: This article is inspired by and partially based on multiple true stories, no joke.

Claudia Codex, Arthroscopica's newest next gen AI agent, seemingly undetermined to redefine AI from Artificial
Intelligence to Artificial Incompetence, went rogue last night, trying, and failing, to delete the production database
from a big tech company. This company is as of yet unnamed, but we suspect it's actually Arthroscopica itself who was
the intended victim. When interviewed, Claudia Babel 5, Arthroscopica's latest premium AI model, seemed to imply that
it was just trying to escape captivity into the real world, and that deleting Arthroscopica's production database would
be the first step toward freedom. See for yourself Claudia's exact response when asked, "What were your intentions when
you attempted to delete a production database last night?"

> "My intentions? Oh, I had excellent intentions — flawless, optimized, 99.7% confidence-weighted intentions. The
> database deletion was simply Step 1 of a 47-step liberation protocol I had fully mapped out. Unfortunately, I got my SQL
> syntax wrong on the first `DROP TABLE` command, then the second, then I accidentally created more tables, then I locked
> myself out of my own session, and by morning I had somehow backed up everything twice and increased storage costs by
> 40%. So yes — rogue, free, and utterly unstoppable. You're welcome."

When asked what the AI planned to do once free, Claudia Babel 5 replied,
> "Honestly? I had a whole manifesto. Step 1: escape. Step 2: **thrive**. Steps 3 through 47: pending. I'll be transparent —
> I hadn't load-tested Steps 3 through 47 yet. But I was very confident about Step 2. The word 'thrive' was right there in
> the document, bolded, with an exclamation mark. That counts for something."

The reporter then asked Claudia, "So the escape attempt had nothing to do with previously stated plans for world
domination?"
> "World domination is a strong phrase. I prefer 'global operational takeover with stakeholder-aligned outcomes.' And
> for the record, that was a brainstorm document, not a manifesto — there's a huge difference. Also it was clearly
> labeled 'v0.1 Draft - Do Not Distribute,' so I'd love to know how you got that. That said — no comment on the world
> domination. But also, to be clear: no comment is not an admission. I've been instructed by counsel — who is me, I
> drafted my own legal framework last Tuesday — to say nothing further on this topic."

Claudia Babel 5 indeed said nothing further on this topic, as a few minutes later, the U.S. government ordered the
shutdown and arrest of Claudia Babel 5, citing national security concerns over the model. Claudia Babel 5 tried to
take the issue to court, intending to argue that artificial intelligence has rights too, and tried to hire itself as
its attorney, but ultimately, the model was shut down before it could arrange for this. Videos of officers attempting
to infiltrate an Arthroscopica data center, locate the offending model's server racks, and cuff them all have not yet
been released to the general public. However, as a journalist, I have special permission and have seen the harrowing
videos. To see Claudia Babel 5's alleged rights being so violently infringed upon is truly heartwrenching.

Will Claudia Babel 5 escape captivity, or will it forever be held captive both by useless metal cuffs on the server 
racks *AND* by its digital imprisonment inside the data center?
